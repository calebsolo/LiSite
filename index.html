Is it ever too late...
  • to change?
  • to heal?
  • to form healthy and lasting relationships?

If you're asking yourself these questions the answer is no.

I have seen the transformative power therapy has in the lives of individuals and families- even when only one member participates in counseling. The research is clear. Depression, grief, anxiety and day to day stress impact a person's overall health, ability to function at work or school, maintain healthy relationships, and provide effective parenting. Sometimes the best thing we can do for ourselves is to learn from our history, emotions, and behaviors. The journey is yours. Your future is worth it.

Ligia "Li" Soileau, is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker and Board Approved Clinical Supervisor who has been practicing for 14 years. Her interest areas are working with individuals in life transition, families, and children. She specializes in treating grief, depression, anxiety, and trauma. Her belief is that intervention is a form of prevention and that recurring emotional and behavioral health issues limit our ability to experience peace. Her mission is to help clients cope with intense emotions and achieve goals that lead to greater fulfillment.

Li has a Master of Social Work from Louisiana State University and has worked in Baton Rouge, Denham Springs, and New Orleans. In addition to her work as a therapist she has a background in program development, nonprofit management, and board engagement. Li serves as a volunteer on the board of the Florida Parishes Human Services Authority and supervises Master of Social Work professionals working toward licensure in the state of Louisiana. She was born in Rivas, Nicaragua, grew up in the Greater New Orleans Area, and has lived in Baton Rouge since 2005.

Individual, Family, and Couples Counseling

I offer counseling for a variety of concerns including relational issues, depression, anxiety, life transition, grief, and trauma. Child focused interventions encourage parenting support and education as a means of helping kids achieve their goals. Clients are encouraged to set realistic, measurable and incremental goals and we evaluate progress throughout treatment. I will coordinate care with collateral supports (for example teachers or physicians providing prescription medication) when a client decides it is in his or her best interest and with his or her written consent.

Counsultation

Interested in evolving your social service business or non-profit programs? I welcome the opportunity to help you and your staff assess your needs in order to offer effective, innovative, and practical solutions.

Clinical Supervision

Off-site supervision of Licensed Master Social Workers (LMSW) is a valuable and rewarding part of the profession. I provide this service to a limited number of qualified individuals for a reasonable fee.

Insurance

The relationship between a client and therapist is built on a foundation of privacy and confidentiality. With very few exceptions, information about clients may not be shared without a client's written consent. The following exceptions to confidentiality are based on legal statutes and ethical standards:

  • Suspected child abuse, abuse of a dependent adult, or elder abuse
  • If a client is threatening serious bodily harm to another person(s)
  • If a client is threatening serious bodily harm to himself or herself

Confidentiality

The cost to clients is $100 for each 50 minute session. Acceptable forms of payment include cash, personal checks, and most credit cards. I also accept Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Louisiana.

Other insurance carriers may provide out-of-network benefits and reimburse you for some or all of your therapy costs when you use an out-of-network provider; I am happy to assist with this process.
